**Real Map Implementation of Bidirectional Dijkstra**

Navigating the urban labyrinth of Mumbai's Colaba, we're showcasing the power of Bidirectional Dijkstra! This algorithm is the ultimate optimizer for finding the shortest path in a weighted graph. Unlike a traditional Dijkstra, which expands outwards from a single source, Bidirectional Dijkstra initiates *two* simultaneous searches: one from the starting point (let's call it the "forward search") and another from the destination point (the "backward search").

The magic happens when these two searches collide in the middle. The algorithm prioritizes exploring neighbors based on their cumulative path cost, effectively expanding outwards in a wave. For efficient neighbor exploration and cost management, it leverages **priority queues** for both the forward and backward searches. These data structures ensure that the nodes with the lowest accumulated distance are always processed first. As a node is visited, its neighbors are evaluated, and their distances are updated if a shorter path is found. The search terminates when a node has been visited by *both* searches, guaranteeing the shortest path with significantly reduced exploration compared to a single-directional search.

This visualization brings graph theory to life! We've transformed the bustling streets and intersections of Colaba into a graph where each **intersection is a node** and each **street segment is a weighted edge**. The weights represent real-world factors like travel time or distance. The **red and green lines** you see are "visited edges" illustrating the expansive exploration of the graph by our forward and backward Dijkstra searches, respectively. When these exploration frontiers meet, the **yellow line** highlights the meticulously computed shortest path. It's crucial to note that real-world road networks present more complex edge weights and potentially asymmetric travel times (e.g., one-way streets) compared to simpler grid-based graphs. This makes Bidirectional Dijkstra's efficiency even more pronounced in these intricate scenarios.
