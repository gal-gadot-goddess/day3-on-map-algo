**Paragraph 1: The Brains Behind the Path – A* Logic**

At its core, A* is an informed search algorithm that excels at finding the shortest path in a weighted graph. It cleverly balances two key pieces of information for each node (intersection in our case): the cost to reach that node from the start (`g(n)`) and an estimated cost to reach the goal from that node (`h(n)`, the heuristic). The magic happens by prioritizing nodes with the lowest `f(n) = g(n) + h(n)`. A* uses a **priority queue** to efficiently manage which nodes to explore next, always picking the one with the lowest `f(n)`. This intelligent heuristic guides the search away from dead ends and towards the goal much faster than algorithms like Dijkstra's (which essentially has `h(n) = 0`, exploring outwards uniformly).

**Paragraph 2: From Streets to Nodes – The Real Map Implementation**

Transforming the intricate network of London's Soho into a graph for A* is a fascinating process. Each **intersection** becomes a **node**, and each **street segment connecting two intersections** becomes a weighted **edge**. The "weights" of these edges can represent travel time, distance, or even traffic conditions! In this visualization, the **red and green lines** you see depict the **visited edges** as A* explores the graph, expanding outwards from our starting point. The **glowing yellow line** is the ultimate **computed shortest path**, found by intelligently navigating this urban maze. Unlike explicit grids, real-world road networks present complexities like one-way streets, varying speed limits, and irregularly shaped blocks, which A* elegantly handles by adapting its heuristics and edge weights.
